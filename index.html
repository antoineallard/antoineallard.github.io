About me:

I am a PhD student in the Physics Department at Université Laval in Québec City, Canada. I am a member of Louis J. Dubé's research group.


Research Interests:

My research is mainly focused on complex network modeling. More specifically, I am interested in developing mathematical models in order to improve our capability to accurately reproduce (and thus hopefully predict) dynamical processes on real complex networks. As a collateral effect, the modeling process by itself is interesting as it highlights fundamental properties of these real systems. Lately, I have been working on extending bond percolation mathematical formalisms to include more realistic complex networks.

I am also interested in Information Theory and Bayesian Inference, although I have not done serious research in that direction. Finally, I find interdisciplinary research, in general, very motivating.
